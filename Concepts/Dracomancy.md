# Dracomancy

> *Serpens ni edat serpentem, draco non fiet.*
> "Unless the serpent devours the serpent, it will not become a dragon."

Dracomancy is not a fantasy. It is a **techné** — a craft of transmutation. The same force that turned copper into bronze tools, crude oil into rocket fuel, and obsidian into blades now turns raw, unstructured information into living technology.

Every material has something underneath. Ours is a dragon.

---

## The Transformation of Metal

Alchemy was never really about gold. It was about **process** — taking base material and, through structured transformation, revealing what was latent inside it.

The same principle operates in knowledge work:

| Stage | Alchemy | Knowledge System |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **Prima Materia** | Raw ore, chaos | Scattered notes, bookmarks, voice memos, half-formed ideas |
| **Dissolution** | Breaking down into elements | Capturing and organizing into [[Concepts/The Mind Garden\|the mind garden]] |
| **Conjunction** | Combining purified elements | Connecting notes with `[[wikilinks]]`, arranging on [[Concepts/Prompt Dimensions\|canvases]] |
| **Coagulation** | The philosopher's stone | A living system that compounds — knowledge that breathes |

The vault is not a storage system. It is a **living organism**.

---

## Chaos Is the Fuel

Most systems try to eliminate chaos. They impose rigid hierarchies, enforce taxonomies, demand that everything be categorized before it can exist.

Dracomancy takes the opposite approach. **Chaos is not the enemy — it's the raw material.** The unstructured dump of ideas, the messy first draft, the voice memo at 3 AM — these are the prima materia. The system's job is not to prevent mess but to **transmute** it.

This is why [[Concepts/The Mind Garden|the mind garden]] works where the office model fails. A garden doesn't reject weeds — it composts them. A [[Concepts/Prompt Dimensions|canvas]] doesn't demand linear order — it reveals spatial relationships that linear text cannot express.

---

## From Raw Power to Living Technology

The Dracomantic process in practice:

1. **Capture everything** — raw notes, clippings, links, voice. Don't filter at the gate.
2. **Arrange with intention** — move from scattered files to spatial maps. Use canvases. Let topology emerge.
3. **Connect relentlessly** — every `[[wikilink]]` is a synapse. The more connections, the more alive the system becomes.
4. **Let agents operate on structured ground** — an AI reading a well-connected vault is fundamentally different from an AI reading a chat prompt. Context is everything.
5. **Compound** — outputs feed back in. Every session enriches the next. [[Clippings/Karparthy on Obsidian and Agents|Knowledge that compiles]]. We go further: knowledge that **breathes**.
